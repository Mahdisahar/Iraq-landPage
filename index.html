Republic of Iraq

Iraq is a country in Western Asia. It is bordered by Turkey to the north, Iran to the east, the Persian Gulf and Kuwait to the southeast, Saudi Arabia to the south, Jordan to the southwest and Syria to the west. The capital and largest city is Baghdad. Iraq is home to diverse ethnic groups including Iraqi Arabs, Kurds, Turkmens, Assyrians, Armenians, Yazidis, Mandaeans, Persians and Shabakis with similarly diverse geography and wildlife. The vast majority of the country's 44 million residents are Muslims – the notable other faiths are Christianity, Yazidism, Mandaeism, Yarsanism and Zoroastrianism.The official languages of Iraq are Arabic and Kurdish; others also recognised in specific regions are Neo-Aramaic, Turkish and Armenian.

Land

Iraq is one of the easternmost countries of the Arab world, located at about the same latitude as the southern United States.

People

Iraq,created by combining three separate Ottoman provinces in the aftermath of World War I is one of the most religiously diverse in the Middle East.

Economy

Iraq economy was based almost exclusively on agriculture until the 1950s, but after the 1958 revolution economic development was considerable.

Government

From 1968 to 2003 Iraq was ruled by the Baʿath Party. Under a provisional constitution adopted by the party in 1970, Iraq was confirmed as a republic
